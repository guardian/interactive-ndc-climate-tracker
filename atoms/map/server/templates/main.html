Majority of countries have improved pledges

According to the Climate Action Tracker, 22 countries out of 40 that were analysed submited stronger NDC targets at Cop26. This included some of the world's largest emitters such as China and the USA.

USA made significant step forward

In what the Climate Action Tracker describes as a "significant step forward", the new US would result in 1.5-2.5 GtCO2e/yr lower emissions in 2030. Although this is still about 5% to 10% points short of a 1.5˚C compatible 2030 target. This means the USA’s pledge is still insufficient.

China moving to net zero before 2060

Similarly, the Chinese pledge has improved at Cop26 but is still deemed to be insufficient for limiting global warming to 1.5˚C.
